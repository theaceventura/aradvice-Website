Implementation vs. Advisory: Knowing the Difference

The distinction between implementation and advisory is often misunderstood. Implementation consultants focus on software deployment and meeting project milestones. They're measured by "on time and on budget" metrics. Advisory consultants focus on risk frameworks, accountability, and legal defensibility. They ensure that when a breach or AI failure occurs, the board can prove it took reasonable steps. Boards often hire implementation firms when they actually require independent governance advisory. This mismatch leaves directors exposed to regulatory scrutiny and wasted investment in "checkbox" compliance reports that fail to address systemic risk.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between an IT consultant and a technology consultant for the board?

An IT consultant typically focuses on technical delivery and system maintenance, while a technology consultant for the board prioritises risk governance and defensible oversight. The latter translates technical data into fiduciary liability. Their role isn't to fix the server but to ensure the board's oversight of that server meets the "Reasonable Steps" test required by ASIC and the standards of the AICD.

Does a technology consultant need to be an expert in every software we use?

No, a board-level advisor focuses on the accountability matrix and strategic alignment rather than specific software mastery. Their value lies in identifying systemic governance gaps and ensuring the C-suite is managing technology within the organisation's risk appetite. They act as a bridge between technical teams and directors, ensuring software choices don't create unmanaged legal or ethical exposures for the firm.

How much does a typical technology consulting engagement cost in Australia?

In May 2026, senior technology consultant rates in Australia range from $250 to over $400 per hour. Specialist contractor day rates for high-stakes roles, such as an LLM Architect or a Program Manager, average between $1,456 and $1,520. These costs reflect the scarcity of the 260,000 skilled professionals currently missing from the Australian tech workforce, particularly in high-demand areas like cybersecurity and AI engineering.

Why should boards hire an independent technology consultant instead of relying on their CISO?

Independence ensures the board receives an objective assessment of the organisation's resilience without the bias of internal politics or self-interest. A CISO is often too close to the technical implementation to provide a truly independent review of their own performance. An external advisor provides a conflict-free perspective, ensuring that management's claims are stress-tested against current regulatory scrutiny and Australian director duties.

What are the legal implications for directors who ignore technology governance advice?

Directors risk personal liability and significant civil penalties under the Corporations Act if they fail to exercise due care regarding technology risk. ASIC has made it clear that "Reasonable Steps" include active oversight of cyber and AI frameworks. Ignoring expert advice can be viewed as a breach of fiduciary duty, especially if a breach occurs and the board cannot prove it maintained a defensible governance structure.

How often should a board engage a technology consultant for a readiness review?

Boards should engage an advisor for a formal readiness review at least annually, or more frequently if the organisation undergoes significant digital transformation. High-risk sectors often adopt a quarterly cadence to keep pace with the 23.64% growth in cloud and platform services. Regular reviews ensure the board's oversight remains current as new regulations, like the Digital Assets Framework commencing April 2027, come into effect.

Can a technology consultant help with AI risk management frameworks?

Yes, a consultant is essential for aligning AI implementation with the National AI Plan released on 2 December 2025. They help directors navigate the "regulation where necessary" approach by integrating AI risk into existing governance frameworks. This ensures that the use of LLMs or automated decision-making systems is transparent, ethical, and compliant with emerging Australian standards and expectations regarding data infrastructure.

What should be included in a technology consultant's engagement letter?

The engagement letter must explicitly define the scope of advisory, the independence of the consultant, and the nature of board-level reporting. It should outline the accountability matrix and specify that the advisor has no conflicts of interest with software vendors. This document serves as part of the board's evidence of diligence, proving they've sought independent, expert guidance to manage their technological fiduciary duties.
