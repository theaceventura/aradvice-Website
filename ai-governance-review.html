AI Governance Board Review

Independent. Conflict-Free. Board-Ready.

AI tools are already inside your organisation, approved or not. This review gives your board a clear, documented oversight position before a regulator, auditor, or incident forces the question.

Who This Is For

psychology

A board whose organisation is adopting AI tools, formally or informally, and has not yet established documented oversight of that adoption.

warning

A board that knows AI is being used across the organisation but has never received a report identifying which tools are in use and what data they process.

gavel

A board in a regulated sector where AI adoption creates specific obligations under the Privacy Act, APRA standards, or sector-specific regulation.

visibility

A board that wants to address its AI governance position before a regulator, an auditor, or an incident raises the question.

What This Review Examines

This is not a technical AI audit. It is an independent assessment of whether your board has defensible oversight of the AI risk your organisation carries.

psychology

AI Exposure

Which AI tools, vendors, and automated systems are operating across your organisation, and whether your board has visibility of them.

gavel

Governance Maturity

Whether your current oversight structure would withstand scrutiny from a regulator, auditor, or court following an AI-related incident.

balance

Director Liability

Where individual directors are exposed under Australian law, and what needs to change to establish a defensible record of oversight.

What You Receive

A formal AI Governance Board Report, delivered to the Chair or Risk Committee and presented in a structured 60-minute debrief session.

check_circle

AI Exposure Register: every AI tool and vendor identified across your organisation, risk-classified, and mapped to accountability. The first time most boards have seen this picture in one place.

check_circle

Governance Maturity Rating: your board's AI oversight scored across five domains against regulatory expectations. With a clear explanation of what a defensible rating requires.

check_circle

Director Liability Assessment: plain-English analysis of where personal exposure exists under Australian law, referenced to specific provisions.

check_circle

Gap-to-Defensible Governance Map: the specific changes required, in the order they should be made, to move your board to a position it can defend.

check_circle

Board Resolution Template: a ready-to-adopt resolution establishing formal AI governance oversight. Adopted, it is itself evidence of active governance.

60-Minute Debrief: a structured session with the Chair or Risk Committee to walk through the findings and confirm the roadmap.
